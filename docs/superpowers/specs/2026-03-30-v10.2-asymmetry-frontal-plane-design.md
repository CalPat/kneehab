# KneeHab v10.2 — Asymmetry Rule + Frontal Plane Rebuild

**Date:** 2026-03-30
**Version bump:** Medium → v10.2
**Base version:** v10.1

---

## Problem

Right-side glute medius weakness creates frontal plane escape during single-leg loading (step down trunk lean, lateral movement difficulty on Cossack/side lunge). Current program has no asymmetry protocol — both sides get identical volume regardless of deficit. Load day Glute block also contains a restorative exercise (Supine Hip ER) that duplicates Recovery day without contributing to pre-activation. Knee block lacks a frontal plane strength exercise between Split Squat and the (now-removed) Skater Squat.

---

## Changes

### 1. Asymmetry Rule — Load Day briefing

Add a named protocol to the Load Day briefing note:

> **Asymmetry Rule:** On all alternating exercises, start with the weaker side. Add one extra set to the weaker side.

Lives in one place. Exercises reference it rather than each repeating the full logic.

---

### 2. Hip Abduction (Glute Block)

**rx change:** `2 × 12 each · 2s hold top` → `3 × 12 weak / 2 × 12 strong · 2s hold top`

**Description addition:** "Start weaker side. Apply asymmetry rule if one side lags in strength or control."

---

### 3. Adductor ISO Hold (Glute Block)

**Description addition:** "Apply asymmetry rule — weaker side first, extra set if one side lags."

---

### 4. Supine Hip ER with Block — removed from Load Day

Restorative deep rotator isometric. Already present on Recovery day. Not a pre-activation stimulus for what follows in the Knee block. Remove from Load Day Glute block to recover ~4 min. Remains on Recovery day unchanged.

Load Day Glute block after change:
- Adductor ISO Hold
- Hip Abduction
- Single-Leg Glute Bridge

---

### 5. Slant Board Step Down — pre-activation cue added

**Description addition:** "Before each set on the weaker side: 5s single-leg stance, focus on level pelvis (glute med actively holding), then begin the descent."

---

### 6. Skater Squat — removed

Full single-leg stability demand exceeds current frontal plane control capacity. Frontal plane strength progression is now: Split Squat → Curtsy Squat → Step Down. Skater Squat removed entirely.

---

### 7. Curtsy Squat — added to Knee block

Replaces Skater Squat. Bilateral base (crossed stance) with high glute med demand in adducted position — trains exactly the deficit pattern. More accessible than Skater Squat; does not require full single-leg stability.

**rx:** `3 × 8 weak / 2 × 8 strong · 2s descent`

**Description:** "Rear foot crosses behind and outside stance foot. Stance-leg glute med resists femoral adduction throughout. Keep stance-leg knee tracking over 2nd–3rd toe — do not let it cave medially. Trunk upright. Apply asymmetry rule — weaker side first."

**Regression:** Reduce depth + hold door frame for balance.
**Progression:** 3–4s eccentric → add light weight (goblet hold).

---

### 8. Cossack Squat — added to Knee block (mandatory)

Lateral movement pattern: hip adductor mobility + frontal plane control under lateral load. Directly addresses the reported deficit in Cossack/side lunge on the right side. Made mandatory (not optional) because the lateral movement deficit is a primary finding.

**rx:** `3 × 5 weak / 2 × 5 strong · 3s descent · 3s ascent`

**Description:** "Feet wider than shoulder-width. Shift weight to one side, bending that knee while the other leg straightens. Toes on bent leg turned out ~15–20°. Stop depth at first sign of trunk lateral lean or knee cave — that is your working depth. Start assisted (door frame or TRX) until pattern is stable. Apply asymmetry rule — weaker side first."

**Regression ladder:**
1. Assisted Cossack (door frame / TRX) — R1, start here
2. Elevated heel Cossack (heel on plate) — removes dorsiflexion restriction
3. Partial depth Cossack — full bodyweight, limited ROM
4. Full Cossack — target
5. Weighted Cossack (goblet hold) — loaded progression

---

### 9. Calf Raise ×2 — moved to Optional pool

Indirect contribution to current deficit. Frees time in Knee block for Curtsy + Cossack. Joins Optional pool (Pick 2) alongside Copenhagen Plank, Hamstring ISO in Flexion, TKE, Single-Leg Wall Sit, Hanging Knee Raise.

---

### 10. Face Pull — removed from Load Day

Scapular correction and shoulder ER maintenance exercise. Already present on Recovery day (full Spinal & Shoulder set). Not a pre-activation stimulus for knee work. Remove from Load Day Spinal & Shoulder block. Saves ~2 min. Remains on Recovery day unchanged.

---

### 11. RKC Plank — removed from Load Day

Full-body co-contraction plank. Already present on Recovery day Core block. Side Plank is more directly relevant to Load day purpose (lateral core stability for frontal plane exercises — Curtsy, Cossack, Step Down). Remove RKC Plank from Load Day Core block. Saves ~1 min. Remains on Recovery day unchanged.

Load Day Core block after change:
- Hollow Body Hold
- Side Plank

---

### 12. Couch Stretch — reduced on Load Day

**rx change:** `2 × 30s each side` → `1 × 30s each side`

Already covered at full volume on Recovery day. One set sufficient as pre-activation before loading. Saves ~30s.
