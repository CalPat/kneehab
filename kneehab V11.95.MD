# kneehab v11.95 — Right-Side Glute Med Bias

**Date:** 2026-05-05
**Type:** Minor change (cue refinements, no new exercises, no schedule changes)
**Predecessor:** v11.94 (Lateral Quad Roll added to primary mobility blocks)

## Diagnostic Context

Three independent findings on the right side, all explained by one upstream cause:

| Finding | Reveals |
|---|---|
| Groin → medial-knee tightness in cat phase of cat-cow and child's-pose-with-reach | Right adductor (gracilis) over-tone — the line is the gracilis distribution from inferior pubic ramus to pes anserine |
| Same line activates during bird dog when right leg is the support leg (left leg lifting) | Right adductor recruited as primary frontal-plane stabilizer instead of glute med + obliques |
| Slant board step-down: left leg tracks straight, right leg can't — compensation present every rep | Right glute med cannot generate enough abduction force to hold the femur in line under single-leg load. Functionally a positive Trendelenburg test under load |

**Root cause (working diagnosis):** Right gluteus medius weakness/inhibition. Adductor group (gracilis especially) compensates by taking on frontal-plane stabilization → chronic over-tone → "tight" feel in stretch and under load. Knee valgus on loading completes the kinetic-chain picture — this is the textbook upstream PFPS pattern.

**Femoral nerve glide test:** tough to duplicate — neural component unlikely.

## Why this is a cue update, not a structural change

The program already contains the right exercises:
- **Hip Abduction** (Day A glute block) — direct glute med isolation
- **Single-Leg Glute Bridge** (Day A glute block) — single-leg posterior chain
- **Slant Board Step Down** (Day A knee block) — exposes single-leg tracking
- **Bird Dog** (Day A and Day B core) — anti-rotation
- **Asymmetry Rule** is already globally stated on Day A: weaker side first, extra set weaker side
- **Soreness Rule** is already globally stated

What was missing: explicit *compensation watch* cues that translate "asymmetry rule" into specific instructions for this dysfunction pattern. This version adds those cues to the four exercises that matter.

## Changes

### Slant Board Step Down (Day A knee block)
Added stance-leg tracking cue + compensation watch list (knee valgus, trunk lean, pelvis drop). Added side-specific regression options:
- Lower slant angle on the failing side only
- Drop to flat step on the failing side only
- Sub Overcoming ISO Step-Up on the failing side only (already a swap in program)
Added mirror/phone visual feedback recommendation.

### Hip Abduction (Day A glute block)
Added "top knee faces forward" cue. If the top knee rolls toward the ceiling on the raise, TFL is recruited instead of glute med. Cue: toes slightly down at the top.

### Single-Leg Glute Bridge (Day A glute block)
Added pelvis-level cue. Unloaded side dropping is the diagnostic signal that the loaded glute med is failing on that side.

### Bird Dog (Day A and Day B core, all 3 variations)
Added compensation watch: if support-leg adductors fire as primary stabilizer (groin to inside-knee tension), pelvis is rotating and obliques aren't engaged. Cue: brace ribcage-to-pelvis first, then lift only as far as support side stays quiet. Quality of stability over range of motion.

## Reassessment Plan (target: 2026-05-19, ~2 weeks)

Re-test on the right:
1. Slant board step-down at full slant angle — does the right knee track straight without compensation?
2. Cat / child's-pose with reach — gracilis-line tightness gone, reduced, or unchanged?
3. Bird-dog support on right — can the lift complete without right-adductor involvement?

Decision tree:
- **All three improved →** return to symmetric programming. Pattern resolved.
- **Step-down clean, but tightness lingers →** reintroduce light targeted medial-thigh work (Cossack stretch) for symptomatic relief. The compensation is gone; residual density is the only remaining issue.
- **Step-down still bad →** escalate. Book PT for hands-on assessment. Could be glute med inhibition (not just weakness), which requires manual therapy + retraining, not more reps.

## Watch for Escalation

Pes anserinus tendinopathy/bursitis (where gracilis + sartorius + semitendinosus converge):
- Focal tenderness 2–3 finger-widths below medial knee joint line
- Pain going up/down stairs, especially down
- Pain when crossing legs

If any of these appear, back off all medial-line stretching and book a PT — that's localized inflammation that needs unloading, not loading.
