# KneeHab V10.1 — Hip Abduction + Copenhagen Plank Added

**Date:** 2026-03-26
**Change type:** Medium (2 exercises added across 2 blocks)

## Summary

Frontal-plane coverage restored and adductor progression pathway completed. Hip Abduction returns to the Glute Activation block (Load day) to address dynamic knee valgus upstream. Copenhagen Plank (short lever) added to Knee-Specific Optional as the Phase 2 adductor progression.

## Changes

### Glute Activation Block (Load Day)
- **Hip Abduction** re-added (2 × 12 each · 2s hold top). Removed in v10.0 to make room for Adductor ISO — restored here as a 4th exercise. Glute medius isolation in the frontal plane. Controls contralateral pelvic drop and femoral adduction/IR under single-leg load. Side-lying, pelvis stacked; band at ankle optional.

### Knee-Specific Optional (Pick 2 — Load Day)
- **Copenhagen Plank — Short Lever** added (3 × 20–30s each side). Phase 2+ exercise — replaces Adductor Side-Lying ISO after 2–4 weeks pain-free. Bottom knee on bench (short lever). Highest hip adductor demand in the program. Do not run concurrently with Adductor ISO. Progression: advance to long lever (ankle on bench) when 3 × 30s manageable.

## Rationale

Both exercises address the hip frontal-plane force couple (abductor–adductor co-contraction) that determines femoral tracking under the pelvis. Abductor weakness → valgus collapse (lateral PF stress). Adductor weakness → medial instability and pes anserine overload. Isometric adductor work was already present (v10.0 Glute block); Copenhagen Plank closes the dynamic loading gap in the progression ladder.

Baar · Bellemans · ATG · Phase 1 / Phase 2 transition
