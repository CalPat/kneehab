# KneeHab V10.21 — Soreness Rule

**Date:** 2026-03-30
**Change type:** Minor

## Summary

Adds the Soreness Rule protocol — a defined modifier for Load days when muscle soreness exceeds 2/10. Replaces dynamic exercises with isometric holds to preserve collagen synthesis stimulus and analgesic benefit (Rio protocol) without aggravating sore tissue. Articular soreness (behind the kneecap or inside the joint) redirects to Recovery day instead.

## Changes

### Load Day briefing
- **Soreness Rule added:** If muscle soreness >2/10, replace dynamic exercises with their isometric modifier. If soreness is articular, switch to Recovery day.

### Isometric modifiers added to 5 exercises (BLOCKS JS + src-knee HTML)
- **Slant Board Step Down:** lower to working angle, hold 30–45s each side
- **Split Squat Progression:** hold at bottom position 30–45s each side
- **Curtsy Squat:** hold in crossed position at working depth 20–30s each side
- **Cossack Squat:** hold at working depth 20–30s each side
- **Hamstring Slide:** skip — substitute Hamstring Isometric in Flexion from Optional pool

### Exercises not requiring modifiers (already isometric)
Wall Sit · Short Arc Quad · Hip Abduction · Adductor ISO Hold · Standing Hamstring ISO Near Extension

## Rationale

Baar — collagen synthesis requires mechanical load; rest drops synthesis.
Rio — isometrics provide analgesic effect and maintain tissue stimulus.
Distinction: muscle soreness (>2/10) → isometric Load day. Articular soreness → Recovery day.
